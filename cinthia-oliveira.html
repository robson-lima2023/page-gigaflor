ABOUT

Research profile

Dr. Cinthia Pereira de Oliveira is a Forest Engineer, researcher and professor with a strong background in geoprocessing, remote sensing, LiDAR, forest measurement and spatial analysis of forest ecosystems. She holds a PhD in Forest Sciences from the Federal Rural University of Pernambuco (UFRPE), where her doctoral research focused on biomass and carbon estimation using conventional forest inventory and LiDAR in tropical dry forests.

Her academic and professional trajectory integrates forest engineering, geosciences and spatial technologies. She has experience in remote sensing, GIS, geoprocessing, multitemporal analysis, forest management, forest measurement and airborne LiDAR applications for biomass and carbon mapping.

Within GigaFlor and PELD-MIAG, Dr. Cinthia contributes to the remote sensing coordination, supporting the integration of field inventories, LiDAR products, spatial datasets and environmental layers to monitor giant Amazonian trees and forest structure across multiple scales.

SCIENTIFIC APPROACH

Remote sensing, LiDAR and forest monitoring

Dr. Cinthia’s work connects field-based forest measurement with spatial technologies to improve the quantification, mapping and monitoring of forest biomass, carbon stocks and land-cover dynamics. Her expertise is particularly relevant for understanding how forest structure varies across environmental gradients and how remote sensing can support long-term ecological monitoring.

Her research experience includes dynamic modeling of forest cover, Google Earth Engine applications, land-use and land-cover change analysis, LiDAR-based estimation of forest attributes, spatialization of biomass and carbon, and multitemporal monitoring of forest landscapes.

By combining geoprocessing, airborne LiDAR, satellite data and forest inventory, her work contributes to the development of spatially explicit tools for conservation planning, forest management, climate-change studies and biodiversity monitoring in Amazonian and seasonally dry tropical ecosystems.

GIGAFLOR CONTRIBUTION

Spatial intelligence for Amazon giant forests

In the GigaFlor network, Dr. Cinthia strengthens the spatial and remote-sensing dimension of the observatory. Her role is essential for transforming field information from giant trees, permanent plots and environmental monitoring into spatial products that can support ecological modeling, conservation decisions and open-science data products.

Her expertise with airborne LiDAR and geospatial analysis supports the mapping of canopy structure, large-tree occurrence, biomass patterns and environmental heterogeneity, providing a key bridge between field ecology and landscape-scale monitoring.
