About GigaFlor

WHO WE ARE

An international observatory for Amazon giant forests

GigaFlor is an international scientific initiative dedicated to understanding, monitoring and conserving the largest trees of Amazonian tropical forests. The observatory integrates long-term ecological monitoring, permanent forest inventories, functional ecology, environmental monitoring, remote sensing and data science to investigate the mechanisms supporting the persistence, resilience and functioning of giant tropical trees.

The initiative emerged from the scientific need to understand how giant emergent trees, frequently exceeding 60, 70 or even 80 meters in height, influence carbon storage, biodiversity, canopy structure, hydrological cycles and climate stability across tropical forests.

Mission

To produce high-quality scientific knowledge on Amazon giant trees through long-term ecological monitoring, forest inventories, functional ecology, environmental monitoring and remote sensing, supporting biodiversity conservation, climate-change mitigation and tropical science.

Vision

To become an international reference in the ecological monitoring of tropical giant trees, promoting scientific innovation, open science, global collaboration and evidence-based conservation of Amazonian forests.
