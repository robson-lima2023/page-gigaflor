ABOUT

Research profile

Dr. Diego Armando Silva da Silva is a Forest Engineer, researcher and professor at the Federal Institute of Amapá (IFAP), Brazil, with extensive expertise in tropical forest ecology, native forest management, biomass dynamics, Amazonian bioeconomy and environmental monitoring.

His research is strongly dedicated to understanding the ecological functioning, conservation and sustainable management of Amazonian forests, with particular emphasis on the ecology and monitoring of giant tropical trees. Over recent years, he has played a key role in scientific expeditions to remote regions of the Brazilian Amazon, especially in the states of Amapá and Pará.

He has contributed to the discovery and scientific documentation of some of the tallest and most extraordinary trees ever recorded in the Amazon Basin, including participation in the efforts that documented the tallest tree identified in the Brazilian Amazon.

SCIENTIFIC APPROACH

Forest ecology, monitoring and Amazonian bioeconomy

Dr. Diego coordinates and collaborates on interdisciplinary projects integrating forest inventories, biodiversity assessment, biomass estimation, carbon dynamics, airborne and terrestrial LiDAR, remote sensing and environmental monitoring.

His work bridges field-based ecological research with advanced spatial technologies, generating scientific evidence to support biodiversity conservation, climate resilience and sustainable forest governance in Amazonian ecosystems.

Beyond ecological research, he is actively engaged in Amazonian bioeconomy initiatives and social technologies designed to strengthen traditional and forest-dependent communities. Among these initiatives, the Kit Solar Castanheiro stands out as an innovative social technology developed to support Brazil nut gatherers and improve energy accessibility and livelihoods in remote forest regions.

EDUCATION AND IMPACT

Training and applied science

As an educator and mentor, Dr. Diego is committed to training the next generation of Amazonian scientists, supervising students and promoting applied research focused on sustainability, conservation and socioecological resilience.

His work contributes to building stronger scientific networks and advancing integrated solutions for biodiversity conservation, environmental monitoring and sustainable development in the Amazon.
