ABOUT

Research profile

Dr. Joselane Pereira Gomes da Silva is a Forest Engineer, professor, and researcher at the State University of Amapá (UEAP), Brazil, with expertise in tropical forest ecology, dendrology, natural regeneration, biodiversity conservation, and ecological monitoring of Amazonian forests.

Her scientific work focuses on understanding forest structure, species composition, regeneration processes, and ecological dynamics that sustain tropical ecosystems under environmental change. Throughout her academic career, she has contributed to research, teaching, extension, and socio-environmental outreach dedicated to strengthening sustainable management, biodiversity conservation, and the appreciation of Amazonian natural heritage.

Within the GigaFlor and PELD-MIAG initiatives, Dr. Joselane plays a key role in the ecological monitoring of giant Amazonian trees, participating in scientific expeditions to remote forests of the Amazon and contributing to the identification, ecological assessment, regeneration studies, and long-term monitoring of exceptional forest specimens.

SCIENTIFIC APPROACH

Forest ecology, dendrology and regeneration

Dr. Joselane’s work integrates field ecology, dendrological knowledge, floristic surveys, natural regeneration studies, and ecological monitoring to understand how tropical forest communities are structured and how they respond to environmental variation, disturbance, and long-term ecological change.

Her contribution to GigaFlor strengthens the ecological interpretation of giant-tree systems by connecting species identification, forest composition, regeneration dynamics, and biodiversity conservation. This perspective is essential for understanding not only the largest trees, but also the ecological processes that allow giant-tree forests to persist across generations.

Her research supports conservation strategies by generating knowledge on forest resilience, biodiversity maintenance, forest dynamics, and ecosystem functioning in landscapes that harbor some of the largest trees on Earth.

GIGAFLOR CONTRIBUTION

Ecological monitoring of Amazon giant trees

In the GigaFlor network, Dr. Joselane contributes to the ecological monitoring of giant trees and associated vegetation, with emphasis on field-based protocols, species identification, dendrology, natural regeneration, and biodiversity assessment.

Her work helps connect the symbolic and ecological importance of Amazon giant trees with broader questions about tropical forest conservation, ecosystem resilience, environmental education, and the long-term valuation of Amazonian biodiversity.
